Background on our instructors

指導員

Rory Daniels
ローリー◦ダニエルズ
3rd grade Black Belt
三段

Having started Shodokan Aikido in 2012, Mr. Daniels has over 9 years of experience under his belt. Mr. Daniels was fortunate to have the tremendous privilege of studying in Spain under Phil Newcombe Sensei before coming to Japan in 2013 - Phil Sensei was a 7th Dan black belt and was arguably the most renowned Shodokan Aikido sensei outside of Japan. After the world Aikido championships in 2013, Mr. Daniels had originally planned to stay and train for a month but after being impressed with the standard of Shodokan Aikido at the Shodokan Headquarters in Japan, he decided to stay for longer to improve his Aikido. In 2016 Mr. Daniels joined the Tenri University Aikido club where he rapidly improved his randori skills. It is largely thanks to that experience that he managed to represent Japan in the 2019 WSAF World Championship in San Diego, USA. There, his team mates and he managed to win gold in the men’s team randori competition and he managed to win silver in the individual men’s randori competition.

After graduating Tenri University in March, 2020, Mr. Daniels assisted in the teaching of Aikido classes for children at Nariyama Shihan’s request, until he moved to Kobe, Japan in November, 2020 for work. Mr. Daniels, through a mutual friend and fellow Aikido practitioner, was then introduced to Mr. Sekiguchi who also wished to practise Aikido in Kobe. It is because of this mutual wish to increase our Aikido training opportunities that we have decided to open an Aikido dojo in Kobe.
