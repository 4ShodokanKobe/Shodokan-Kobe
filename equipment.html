EQUIPMENT

For trial participation

For first timers, it is not necessary to purchase any equipment until you decide to join us. Until then, for those who wish to try it out, please bring loose fitting sport-worthy clothes and something with which to rehydrate.
No belts or other accessories & jewelry are to be worn during practice. That is both for your safety and that of those who practice with you.

Once you decide to join us

The clothing that Shodokan practicioners wear is called a 'dogi' or 'judogi' - Not to be misconstrued with the much lighter, thinner and easier to tear 'kimono'. We recommend purchasing your first 'dogi' through the Shodokan Honbu headquaters but should you wish to purchase one elsewhere that would not be an issue. (The Shodokan Aikido emblem and mark are included on all dogi bought through the Shodokan Aikido headquaters). Please contact us for more details and prices.

Belts

The Shodokan Aikido syllabus designates white belts for beginners; blue for those who pass kyu grades 8 though 6, green for kyu grades 5 through 3; brown for kyu grades 2 and 1; black for 'shodan'(first degree) and above.

Weapons

The soft 'tanto'

The soft tanto, which is often referred to as 'tanto' is a foam item that is designed to resemble a dagger. We use this frequently in our training as a means to learn avoidance and how to counter knife stabs. Tantos are purchased and provided by the club so you will never be asked to purchase them.

The 'Bokuto' (Aka. Wooden Sword)

These are used in syllabus gradings for 2nd degree and 3rd degree black belts but are not required purchases until then. However, we plan on frequently practicing 'bokuto' use and kata. We will share ours with you in such circumstance. If you wish to purchase your own then we can help find the right one for you. Instructor Sekiguchi is a sword master whose help in the matter would prove invaluable.

'Jo' (Aka. spear or staff)

Like 'bokuto' these are also used in syllabus gradings for 2nd degree and 3rd degree black belts. Although we will likely practice with these from time to time, their usage will be less common than that of 'bokuto'.

The 'Hakama'

Although these are used in other Aikido styles we do not usually use them ourselves as we emphasise a lot of our training on randori (sparring with resistance) techniques. Hakama do tend to hinder our mobility and do prove dangerous if worn during randori practise.

First timers

Is it your first time in a dojo? Are you worried about not knowing what's what? Well please be assured that is not an issue! If ever you are unsure about something then please feel free to either ask us questions in person or send them to our email addresses.
We are here to help!

Masks

For the duration of the SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) pandemic we would like to ask practitioners to wear face masks when training with another person.
